Security

Built like the banks. Run like software.

Polish is nice. Specifics are better. Here is exactly how your money and data are protected.

Encryption, both directions

AES-256 at rest, TLS 1.3 in transit. Card numbers are tokenised — full PANs never touch our application servers. Keys are rotated quarterly in dedicated HSMs.

Strong Customer Authentication

Every payment above €30 requires SCA per PSD2 — biometric or hardware-key confirmation in the app. New payees are verified with IBAN-name matching before the first cent moves.

Audited, externally

SOC 2 Type II renewed annually by Lindqvist & Brouwer Assurance. Penetration tests twice a year by Helder Security (Amsterdam). Reports available under NDA to any customer.

Bug bounty, in public

A standing bounty program with payouts up to €25,000 for critical findings. 142 reports triaged in 2025, median first response under 6 hours. Researchers are credited, not threatened.

Straight answers

The questions you should be asking.

What happens to my money if Brightvault shuts down?

Honestly: your deposits don't sit with "Brightvault the app." They're held at our partner bank, ClearLedger Bank N.V., where they're protected up to €100,000 per business under the EU Deposit Guarantee Scheme. If Brightvault disappeared tomorrow, the scheme guarantees repayment within 7 working days. Anything above €100,000 would rank as a claim on the bank — which is why we tell customers holding more to ask us about balance-sweeping across partner banks.

Is the 3.8% rate guaranteed?

No — and be wary of anyone who says theirs is. It's a variable rate that tracks ECB policy. What we do guarantee: every rate change is announced 30 days before it takes effect, applied to everyone equally, and shown in the app with the date it changes. No teaser rates, no "new customers only."

Is Brightvault an actual bank?

No. Brightvault Technologies B.V. is a licensed payment institution under PSD2, supervised by De Nederlandsche Bank. Your deposits are held at ClearLedger Bank N.V., a fully licensed EU credit institution. This split is standard for modern business banking — you get the deposit protection of a bank with software a bank would take a decade to ship.

Ten minutes to open an account — really?

Median time from "start" to "active IBAN" was 9 minutes 40 seconds last quarter, for sole proprietors with an EU ID. Multi-shareholder companies take longer — typically 1–2 business days, because we verify every person owning 25%+ (that's anti-money-laundering law, not us being slow). You can prepare invoices and buckets while verification runs.

What does it actually cost? Where's the catch?

Free is €0 forever; Scale is €9/month flat. The full fee table is published above — FX at 0.5% (0.25% on Scale) and ATM withdrawals past your free allowance at €1 are the only per-use charges. We make money on plan fees, interchange when you pay by card, and the margin between wholesale rates and the 3.8% we pass to you. That's the whole model.

How hard is it to leave?

Two clicks, in the app: Settings → Close account. Remaining balance transfers out same-day to any IBAN you name, statements are exported as PDF/CSV before closure, and there is no exit fee. Around 1.1% of customers leave per year; we'd rather earn the other 98.9% than lock anyone in.

Do you sell my transaction data?

No. Not anonymised, not aggregated, not to "trusted partners." Our revenue is plan fees, interchange, and deposit margin — listed in the answer above. Your data is processed under GDPR within the EU, and you can export or request deletion of everything we hold from inside the app.
