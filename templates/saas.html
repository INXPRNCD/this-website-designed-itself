What Loopline does

One train. Everything green rides. Everything else waits.

Loopline sits on top of your existing CI — GitHub Actions, GitLab, CircleCI — and turns scattered deploys into a schedule your whole company can read.

Release trains, on rails

Pick a cadence — daily at 16:00, every Friday at 17:00, whatever fits. Loopline gathers every merge that passed checks, sequences services by dependency, and departs on time. Miss the train? Your change rides the next one. No pings, no “can someone deploy?”

Rollback in one click

Every train keeps its last-good snapshot warm. One click reverts artifacts, config, and feature flags together — median 38 seconds across all customers, measured, not promised.

Environment protection rules

Declare what production demands — approvals, soak time, error budgets — once, in plain YAML. Loopline enforces it on every train, including the 2 a.m. hotfix.

Before you object

The questions every platform lead asks

What if a release goes wrong mid-train?

The train holds itself. Canary metrics are compared against your error budget every 10 seconds; a breach pauses promotion, pages the on-call, and arms one-click rollback of the entire batch — artifacts, config, and flags together. Median time back to last-good across all customers: 38 seconds.

We already have CI/CD in GitHub Actions. Why add another tool?

Keep it — Loopline doesn’t replace your CI, it orchestrates it. Your Actions still build and test; Loopline decides what ships together, when, in what order, and how it comes back if it shouldn’t have. Nothing in your .github/workflows changes on day one.

Won’t release trains slow my fastest engineers down?

The opposite, in practice. With daily or twice-daily trains, the median wait for a merged change is under 4 hours — and urgent fixes can take a logged, single-approval express lane. What disappears is the slow part: hand-run deploys, Slack-thread approvals, and the Monday pile-up after a freeze.

How long does setup actually take?

Median is 45 minutes from signup to first scheduled train, because Loopline reads the pipelines you already have. The longest part is usually deciding your cadence. Fernwheel (84 engineers, 23 services) was fully migrated in two afternoons.

What happens to our data if we cancel?

You cancel in two clicks from Settings — no sales call. Within the hour you get a complete export: audit log (JSON), release history, and configs. We keep your data 30 days in case you change your mind, then delete it and confirm in writing.

Is Loopline compliant enough for our auditors?

SOC 2 Type II (report available under NDA), ISO 27001, GDPR with EU hosting in Frankfurt on the Enterprise plan. The immutable audit log maps releases to approvals to individuals — Stackhouse cut their change-management evidence gathering from a week to one afternoon.
