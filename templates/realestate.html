Fair questions

What sellers ask us first.

Why not just list on the big portals myself?

You can — and for some properties it is the right call. But portals optimise for clicks, not for price. A public listing invites bargain hunters, burns your home’s freshness in three weeks, and tells every neighbour your plans.

Our register-first approach reverses that: vetted buyers compete privately before any public exposure. It is why our average sale closes at 98.2% of asking in 23 days, while the Hamburg portal average sits near 94% in nine weeks.

What does the valuation cost — and what’s the catch?

Nothing, and there isn’t one. The visit, the comparable-sales analysis and the written report are free, whether or not you ever sell. We invest the afternoon because roughly one in three owners returns to us — some after four years. We are patient.

We’re not sure we want to sell yet. Is it too early to talk?

It is exactly the right time. A confidential valuation gives you a real number for estate planning, refinancing or simply peace of mind — with zero obligation and zero visibility. Most of the owners we value in spring are not sellers. Some become sellers years later; many never do. Both outcomes are fine.

How do you keep a sale genuinely discreet?

No garden sign, no portal listing, no open house. Buyers from our register sign a confidentiality undertaking before they receive the address. Viewings are scheduled singly and accompanied by your partner — never a junior with a key box. Six in ten of our 2025 sales were never publicly visible.

What are your fees?

A success fee only, agreed in writing before we start and due solely on notarised completion — no sale, no fee, no marketing costs passed on. We quote the exact rate at the valuation, because it depends on the property, not on a rate card. And there are no exclusivity traps: our mandate can be ended with two weeks’ notice, in two sentences.
